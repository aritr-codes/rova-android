# Rova Accessibility Audit — WCAG 2.2 AA (2026-05-29)

Audit of the Rova Android app (`com.aritr.rova`) against WCAG 2.2 Level AA, covering 10 UI surfaces plus 2 cross-cutting concerns (design tokens, motion). Findings are normalized from 12 audit agents. Severities use Blocker > Serious > Moderate > Advisory. A handful of contrast severities were normalized for consistency where the same alpha value was scored differently across surfaces (e.g. the 0.36-alpha progress header is treated as Blocker per the recovery agent's escalation; 0.45-alpha body text as Serious unless on the warning sheet where it is the gating block).

> **Note on small-text contrast:** the notification agent scored against a 7:1 bar. WCAG 2.2 AA requires 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text; 7:1 is the AAA bar. These findings are retained as Serious because the measured ratios (3.6–4.97:1) also fail or sit at the edge of the 4.5:1 AA line for the small (8–12sp) sizes used.

Overall posture: **failing**. Three Blockers, ~58 Serious, ~45 Moderate, ~11 Advisory. Defects cluster into four root causes: (1) low-alpha text tokens, (2) missing screen-reader semantics on custom clickable Rows, (3) no reduced-motion gating, (4) undersized touch targets. Fixing the token layer (`RecordChromeTokens.kt`, `SettingsSheetTokens.kt`) and the motion helper (`RovaAnimations.kt`) resolves a disproportionate share of findings.

## record (RecordScreen)

RecordScreen has critical color-contrast failures in text-on-transparent chrome (nav labels, status detail, settings-card keys, zone tags) violating 1.4.3, plus non-text-contrast failures on focus brackets, grid, and dividers (1.4.11).
