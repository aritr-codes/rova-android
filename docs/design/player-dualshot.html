Rova · Player Redesign — "Playback to Standard"

Player DualShot — the two-angle playback contract

Everything unique to a DualShot (P+L) recording in the player: how you know which angle you're watching, how you switch to the other, what happens when a side is unavailable, how continuity and per-side resume behave, and how kept-raw DualShot is treated. Its load-bearing job is to settle RK3 — the minter-contract decision player-core and player-states both defer here. The answer, forced by the repo: a DualShot session is two independent files under one sessionId, and switching angle is an artifact-selection over a Library-minted pair — never an in-player synthesis of identity (that would break the ADR-0037 sole-minter invariant) and never a render (no compositor exists). It layers on the seven frozen specs, preserves ADR-0037/0038/0032/0025, and the in-player switch is PROPOSED, gated behind an RK3 ADR amendment that widens the transport — no schema change, no backend invented.

00

Model & boundary — what a DualShot recording is, and the wall it stands behind

The one-line model, the sole-minter invariant it must not break, the backbone it preserves, and the lines it may not cross.
The model in one line

A DualShot recording is two independent video files — Portrait and Landscape — that share one sessionId, recorded together but finalized separately. The player always shows exactly one side at a time; this spec owns knowing which and switching between them.

Switching angle is choosing which already-resolved artifact to play — an artifact-selection problem, not a rendering problem. Nothing is composited, stitched, or re-encoded; the two files are never combined.

The hard constraint — the sole-minter invariant
Identity is transported, never reconstructed

ADR-0037: only the Library may mint a playback identity. The player receives (sessionId, side?, segmentIndex?) and passes it verbatim; it may not synthesize the sibling side itself. So "switch to the other angle" cannot be the player quietly flipping side — that would be the player minting identity.

This is the whole of RK3 (§03). The resolution: the Library mints both side identities and transports them as a pair; the player only selects the active member. In-player synthesis is Rejected.

Frozen · what DualShot playback may NOT touch
  • Never reconstruct identity. The player never synthesizes the sibling side; both identities are Library-minted and transported (ADR-0037). Switching selects a pre-minted member — it does not derive one.
  • Never composite / render. No side-by-side, no picture-in-picture, no stitched stream — the app has no compositor or transcode (that is the Rejected editor). One side plays; the other waits.
  • Never redesign the player, transport, timeline, gestures, actions, sharing, info, or editing — those are the seven frozen specs. This spec adds only the angle indicator + switch and the DualShot-specific states.
  • Never invent backend. No new manifest schema field, no per-side exportState enum, no new dependency. The switch reuses the existing resolver, per-side pointers, per-side resume, and the side-keyed lease.

02

DualShot philosophy — two truths, never a fake third

First principles for a recorder that captured two real angles the user didn't frame — filtered through "one at a time · never fabricate · never combine."

DualShot exists because the user asked Rova to keep two vantage points of the same unattended moment. Both are real footage; neither is primary. The player's duty is to make both reachable and legible without ever pretending they are one thing. Multi-angle products split on how honest they are about the seams:

SourceWhat they doPrinciple Rova takes
Insta360 / GoPro (multi-lens)Reframe/switch angle at the same timestamp; one continuous view, the other angle a tap away.Adopt same-moment angle switch — the playhead is preserved, switching feels like turning your head, not opening a new video.
DJI (dual/multi-cam)Offer explicit per-camera clips; each is its own file you pick from.Adopt each angle is its own artifact — exactly Rova's two-file truth; switching is selection, not synthesis.
Blackmagic CameraSingle high-craft stream; no consumer multi-angle switch.Note confirms multi-angle is a consumer review affordance, not a production one — keep it calm and few-tap.
Any "grid / side-by-side" multi-viewRender both angles at once in a composed layout.Reject a composed render Rova cannot produce (no compositor) and does not want — it dilutes both angles and invents a third artifact.
Frozen philosophy
  • Two real files, one on screen. The player shows one angle at a time. Switching selects the other; it never composites, splits the screen, or plays both.
  • Same moment across the switch. Switching preserves the playhead (both sides share one recording wall-clock, ADR-0032) — you keep watching the same instant from the other angle (§05).
  • Never fabricate a missing side. Sides finalize independently; if one didn't finish, the player says so plainly and offers no switch — it never shows a black or fabricated frame for it (§06).
  • Selection, never minting. The two identities are Library-minted and transported; the player chooses between them. This is the RK3 line and it is non-negotiable (§03).
03

Identity model — settling RK3, the minter-contract decision

The question player-core deferred here: who mints the sibling-side identity on a switch? Answer, forced by the repo: the Library, as a transported pair. Never the player.

The repo fact is blunt: PlaybackIdentity is not a type — it is the tuple (sessionId, side?, segmentIndex?) travelling as three real values from the Library's nav call, through the PlayerViewModel constructor, into the resolver. side is fixed at construction and is part of the ExoPlayer lease key. There is no switchSide anywhere. So an in-player angle switch is a genuinely new capability, and its only real design question is who is allowed to produce the second side's identity.

RK3 optionMechanismVerdict
Player flips side itselfOn a switch, the player sets side = the other value and re-resolves from the already-loaded manifest.Rejected This is the player synthesizing identity — the exact thing ADR-0037:143-158 forbids ("no subsystem other than the Library may synthesize a playback identity"). Convenient, but it breaks the invariant. Not adopted.
Library mints & transports the PAIRWhen a DualShot session is opened, the Library (which already knows the session→artifact fan-out and already mints per-side rows) mints both side identities and transports them together, one marked active. The player holds the pair and selects the active member on switch.Adopt Identity stays Library-minted and transported whole (ADR-0037 intact); switching is pure selection, no synthesis. This is the RK3 resolution.
Bounce to the Library to re-open the other sideNo in-player switch; the user backs out and opens the sibling row.Interim Correct and shippable today with zero change — the Library already renders per-side rows. This is the honest behavior until the pair-transport lands.
Frozen decision · RK3 settled

In-player angle switching is an artifact-selection over a Library-minted, transported pair of identities. The Library — the sole minter — produces both (sessionId, PORTRAIT) and (sessionId, LANDSCAPE) and hands them to the player together; the player selects the active side and never synthesizes the sibling. The player flipping side on its own is Rejected as a sole-minter violation. Until the pair-transport is built, the interim, shipping-honest behavior is: no in-player switch — the user returns to the Library (which already lists both sides) to open the other angle. Either way, identity is transported, never reconstructed.

Why the pair, not a URI (the sharing/info precedent doesn't transfer)

player-sharing and player-info read the other side's manifest pointer for a one-shot action (send a URI / show a size) without minting a full identity — and that is correct for them. Angle switch is different: it plays the other side, which needs a full leased player keyed on that side's identity (ADR-0038). A played side must be a real transported identity, not a URI the player conjured — so it must come from the Library. That asymmetry is the whole reason RK3 needed settling here rather than being borrowed from sharing.

04

Angle switching — the interaction, and the one honest capability gap

Where the control lives, what it does, and exactly what must be built (classified Existing · Future · Rejected) to ship it.
The interaction
  • A segmented Portrait | Landscape selector in the top overlay (player-core's wayfinding band), reading the current side from the active transported identity. Quiet, ink-dim, auto-hides with the chrome.
  • Tap the other side → play it from the same moment (§05). One tap, no confirmation — switching is safe and reversible (tap back).
  • The indicator is always shown for P+L; the switch is shown only when both sides are playable. One-sided → it degrades to a plain "Portrait only" label (§06). Present-not-disabled, exactly like the sharing side sheet.
  • Single-mode recordings show none of this — no angle chrome at all. This is strictly a P+L surface.
What must be built — classified
Per-side resolve from one manifestExistingresolve(manifest, side) already returns each side (PlayerUriResolver.kt:167-212).
Side-keyed leased playerExistingThe lease key already varies by side (ADR-0038; PlayerScreen.kt:120-123).
Per-side resume storageExistingpositionsBySide keyed by slotFor(side, …).
Library mints + transports BOTH sidesFuture (light)Extends the transport (nav args / VM inputs) to carry the pair. No schema change — the manifest already holds both sides' pointers. RK3 ADR amendment + owner sign-off.
Player-synthesizes-sibling-sideRejectedSole-minter violation (§03).
Composite / side-by-side / PiP outputRejectedA render Rova can't do; two files stay two files (§02).
Frozen decision · the switch is selection, and the only new part is transport

Everything the switch plays already exists — the resolver, the side-keyed lease, per-side resume. The only new machinery is the Library minting and transporting both side identities so the player can select between them without synthesizing one. That is a transport widening, not a schema change and not a backend, and it is the sole thing gating in-player switching. It is Future, behind the RK3 ADR amendment + owner sign-off; the angle indicator and the Library-bounce interim ship without it.

Frozen decision · continuity by wall-clock, resume stays per-side

Switching angle carries the live playhead to the other side (same wall-clock moment, clamped to its duration) via a fresh side-keyed lease (ADR-0038), preserving play/pause and never autoplaying. It does not consult the other side's stored resume — that value stays reserved for re-opening from the Library, and each side continues to persist its own positionsBySide entry. Unequal side lengths are shown truthfully (final frame, paused), never padded. This spec adds no new persistence; it only defines which existing position a switch reads.

Frozen decision · missing angles are named, kept-raw has no swap

A side with no finalized artifact is absent from the switch (present-not-disabled, mirroring the sharing side sheet), and the player states plainly which angle is missing and why — it never fabricates a frame for it. Kept-raw DualShot offers no angle switch at all: an interrupted session has no two finalized side files, so its interleaved kept clips are navigated as clips (each a V2 keptsegment:// resolve), not angles. Every unavailable path routes through the resolver's existing fail-closed Unavailable state; this spec invents no new recovery machinery and no new terminal state.

08

Motion — a cut between angles, not a transition between videos

Switching should feel instant and same-moment. Reuse the inherited chrome timings; add no bespoke transition.
Motion (inherited only)
  • The angle switch is a hard cut at the same moment — no crossfade, slide, or flip. Turning your head is instantaneous; so is this.
  • The selector's selected pill moves on the inherited micro timing (--t-micro, --ease-std) — the same small state-change motion the rest of the chrome uses.
  • The top overlay itself auto-hides/shows on player-gestures' existing chrome motion — the angle control rides that, adding nothing.
  • No new motion token. Anything that seems to need one is a player-core change first, gated behind the RK3 ADR.
Why a cut, not a transition

A crossfade would imply the two angles are being blended — the exact composited-render idea §02 rejects. A hard cut says "same moment, other camera," which is the truth. It also sidesteps any illusion that a switch is loading a new video; it is selecting an already-resolved one at the same instant.

09

Reduced motion — nothing to remove, because there was nothing to add

The inherited invariant, trivially satisfied: the switch is already a cut.
  • Under rememberReduceMotion(), the angle switch is unchanged — it was already an instant cut, with no animation to strip.
  • The selected-pill micro-motion becomes an instant state change; the pill still shows the current side.
  • The top-overlay show/hide follows the reduced-motion rule the gesture spec already defines (appears without slide).
  • No part of angle switching is discoverable only through motion — which side is on screen, and which you can switch to, are legible from static state alone. Same rule as every other player spec: animation changes, behavior and content do not.

Frozen boundary — what this spec owns, what stays elsewhere, what needs an ADR

DualShot playback owns the angle indicator, the switch, and the two-angle states. The transport widening is Future behind the RK3 ADR.
player-dualshot.html OWNS
  • The angle identity display — which side is on screen — in the top overlay.
  • The angle-switch interaction (§04): the Portrait|Landscape selector, one-tap same-moment switch, present-not-disabled.
  • The RK3 resolution (§03): switch = selection over a Library-minted transported pair; in-player synthesis Rejected.
  • The continuity + per-side resume semantics across a switch (§05) and the unavailable-side / kept-raw DualShot states (§06).
  • The DualShot a11y + motion contract for the above.
NOT owned here (do not design)
  • Transport, timeline, gestures, auto-hide, the core layout — player-core / player-gestures. The angle control rides them.
  • The Loading/Unavailable stream states a switched-to side may enter — player-states (it designs the swapped stream's own states when this lands).
  • Which side(s) to share — player-sharing's side sheet. Describing both sides — player-info's Angles group. Trimming one side — player-editing.
  • The Library's per-side rows and the actual pair-minting code — Library layer (ADR-0037 owner). This spec specifies the transported contract, not its implementation.
  • Any composited/multi-view surface — Rejected, not deferred.
Boundary rule

This spec settles RK3 and defines the experience of watching and switching between a DualShot session's two angles, so that — if approved — it drops onto Rova's real grain (two files, one sessionId, one manifest with per-side pointers, per-side resume, side-keyed leases). It consumes the Library-minted identities (ADR-0037), the leased player (ADR-0038), and the wall-clock playhead (ADR-0032) — and redefines none of them. The DualShot contract — two independent files · one on screen · switch = selection over a Library-minted transported pair · never synthesize identity · never composite · same-moment continuity · per-side resume · unavailable sides named not faked · kept-raw has no swap — is frozen here; a change routes back through this HTML and is re-approved first. The angle indicator and the Library-bounce interim ship today; the in-player switch remains PROPOSED, gated behind the RK3 ADR amendment (Library pair-transport — a transport widening, no schema change) and owner sign-off before any Compose work.
