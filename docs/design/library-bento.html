v3.1 · pre-freeze usability pass: day headers are now two-state — plain content at rest, glass veil only while actually pinned, native push-off by the next day (kills the "persistent chrome strip" feel without losing context) · orientation made legible everywhere: the meta pill leads with the recording's shape (single = ▯ or ▭; DualShot = ▯▭ in pane order, Portrait always left), echoed in sheet facts, player subtitle, reader labels, and an aspect-honest player frame · DualShot consistency: panes share the single-tile loading placeholder, seam tokenized as --media-seam. No treatment added to mute the diptych — its salience is information (two recordings, two tap targets), and singles stay content-first.
v3.2 · header de-chroming (owner round 2): the pinned day header is no longer glass — no blur, no border, no surface fill, no bottom edge. While pinned, the page's own color (--bgTop) dissolves over the exit edge (solid behind the label, ~34px fade tail), so the timeline itself appears to carry the date label; anatomy, typography and text colors are identical in both states, and every palette washes with its own ground (Daylight = paper, Eclipse = near-black). Fully-naked pinned text was evaluated and fails — themed text over arbitrary thumbnails has unbounded contrast, and a text-shadow halo can't save the 11.5px dim meta line. --chrome token retired (selection bar/toast/rail keep --chrome-solid). codex round: wash primed synchronously at render (a pinned header never paints naked over thumbnails, IO alone lands a frame late) + wash geometry locked to explicit text metrics (21px line-heights → 37px header box, 31px solid hold, 63px total).
v3.2.1 · frozen (post-freeze correction): sheet action relabeled Share (was "Export" — ADR-0030 §1: export is not a Library action; Share hands a content-URI to another app and saves nothing, matching shipped behavior). Label-only fix at Compose transcription kickoff.
