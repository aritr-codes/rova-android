Rova · Player Redesign — "Playback to Standard"

Player Core — the playback foundation

The token-governed, dark, over-media shell every later player spec inherits: layout, information hierarchy, chrome, glass/over-media substrate, typography, spacing, motion, control and timeline placement, immersive behavior, and interaction philosophy. Grounded in rova-player-discovery.md and the current source — not memory. Preserves the ADR-0037 identity backbone, ADR-0038 lifecycle ownership, ADR-0032 wall-clock playhead, the segmented timeline, the NO-autoplay trust rule, and WCAG 2.2 AA by default. This document intentionally excludes loading/unavailable/retry, gestures, share, keep/discard, DualShot, angle-swap, and fit/fill — each is its own spec.

Specimen
Dark-only, over-media · single surface
00

Design principles

The foundation. Every later player spec obeys these.
The ten
  • Review, not consumption. Deliberate inspection of the user's own footage. Press-to-play, never autoplay. No feed grammar.
  • The session timeline is the spine. Continuous scrub + segment ticks + clip-jump is Rova's pro-review edge — the PRIMARY navigation, not the ±seek. Session boundaries are deliberate walls.
  • On the token system, honestly dark. The player joins RovaTrustTokens/RovaMotion/4dp-grid. It is a legitimately dark-only, over-media surface — modelled on the Trust pinned precedent, not a second hand-rolled theme. Zero new Color.White.copy(alpha).
  • Metadata is navigational and quiet. "Clip N of M · time-of-day" is wayfinding → legible in a quiet overlay; everything else is on demand.
  • Immersive by default. The video is the hero; chrome is a guest that arrives on tap and leaves on a timer. Two localized gradient bands, never a full-frame wash.
  • Every gesture has a visible control. Onboarding-free. Tap/double-tap are accelerators, never the only path. Seekbar semantics preserved.
  • One primary, neutral. Exactly one obvious transport control; no button row of coloured equals. Colour would fight arbitrary footage.
  • Respect the physics. No autoplay, no MediaSession, no frame-step, no editor in V1 (Media3 1.4.1 / trust / scope).
  • Accessibility is the floor. WCAG 2.2 AA by default, reduced-motion gated, 48dp targets, over-media focus in WHITE.
  • Chrome is policy, not hardcode. Visibility runs through AutoHideChromePolicy — TalkBack-suppressed, reduce-motion-gated. The shell renders within it; it never overrides it.
What this shell fixes

The player is the one major screen that opts out of the token system — 3 token references in the whole screens/player/* tree, all in SegmentedTimeline.kt. Chrome is built from 11 hand-tuned Color.White.copy(alpha=…) literals and Color.Black gradients. Contrast correctness is per-literal, not centrally enforceable.

This spec re-expresses the identical visual result on the Trust over-media token family — same white-on-near-black contract already swept by TrustContrastSweepTest. The redesign is a transcription, not a re-plumbing: the ADR-0037/0038/0032 backbone is untouched.

Non-negotiable
NO autoplay · identity minted by Library and transported whole (ADR-0037) · resolver fails closed · fresh player per lease + ownership-gated mutation (ADR-0038) · Media3 1.4.1 envelope · dark-only over-media token model.

Anatomy · top → bottom
Video surface
Full-bleed PlayerView (TextureView) on Color.Black. Entry poster (ContentScale.Fit) painted over black until first frame or the 1500ms ceiling. Middle of frame carries no scrim — the hero zone.
Top overlay
96dp band, --scrim-top (black .82→0). Back (40dp glyph, 48dp target) + two stacked titles: primary date/time (--media-ink) · sub "N clips · total" (--media-ink-dim). Quiet, navigational, nothing more.
InfoRow
Two lines, 4dp gap. L1: position --media-ink-dim · Clip N of M --media-ink. L2: wall-clock time-of-day --media-ink · gap chip --media-ink-body. Tabular-nums.
Timeline
The spine. 3dp bar, 2dp radius, track .18 / fill .90 (1.0 scrubbing), 1px boundary ticks at .40. Fill animates on containerSpring(); snap() while scrubbing or reduce-motion.
Controls
Bottom-anchored cluster, 24dp gaps. −10s · play/pause (48dp pinned pill, --glyph-fill) · +10s. Speed chip pinned to CenterEnd (48dp, cycles 0.5×→2×). One neutral primary; no colour.
Scrims
Two localized bands only (--scrim-top / --scrim-bottom). Never a full-frame wash — the video stays the hero (competitive REJECT).
Frozen rationale — bottom cluster, not center-frame transport
Apple Photos moved play/pause to center-frame. Rova deliberately keeps the transport bottom-anchored, clustered with the timeline (closer to Google Photos / Blackmagic). Reason: the segmented timeline is the spine, and controls belong beside their scrubber; the center frame is reserved for the tap-toggle target and the double-tap seek zones (defined in player-gestures.html), which must stay unobstructed. A persistent center button would collide with those zones and occlude the hero. One primary, near-monochrome, beside the spine.

Layout & information hierarchy

Three zones. The middle is sacred.
ZoneCarriesInkRule
Top overlay (96dp)Back · primary date/time · "N clips · total"ink / ink-dimQuiet wayfinding only. No actions beyond Back in core (share/overflow arrive in player-actions).
Hero (middle)The video—No scrim, no persistent chrome. Tap target + double-tap zones live here (gestures spec). Never occlude.
Bottom panelInfoRow → SegmentedTimeline → ControlsRowink / ink-dim / ink-bodyOrder fixed. Timeline is the spine, sandwiched by its readout (above) and its transport (below).

Information priority (loudest → quietest): Clip N of M & timeline position (navigational, --media-ink) → date/time & wall-clock (context, ink) → per-clip length & gap chip (detail, ink-dim/body). Nothing floats mid-frame; everything competes for the edges only (Blackmagic/Kino principle). Load-bearing metadata is navigational — where am I in the session — so it earns the brightest ink; decorative detail recedes.

04

Chrome & immersive behavior

Rendered within AutoHideChromePolicy. This shell never overrides the policy.
Visibility rules
  • Immersive by default. Chrome present on entry, then auto-hides 3000ms after playback starts.
  • Tap toggles both bands together (fade --t-small 200ms, --ease-std). Reduce-motion → instant, no fade.
  • Chrome persists while paused — a stopped frame is a decision point, not immersive playback.
  • Scrubber is last to hide / first to show — the spine outlives the transport glyphs (competitive ADOPT).
  • TalkBack suppresses auto-hide entirely — removing a focused control traps focus (existing policy, preserved).
  • Both bands are one visibility unit; they never desync.
Substrate — how chrome stays legible over ANY frame
  • Two localized gradient bands (top .82→0 · bottom .90→0), never a full-frame wash.
  • Over-media text uses the locked white ink family, modelled on the Trust over-media sweep discipline. Caveat: the shipped TrustContrastSweepTest proves these inks against opaque pinned surfaces — it does not cover a translucent scrim over arbitrary (worst-case bright) video, nor the faded gradient edges. So this shell requires a new scrim-composite worst-case pin (§07), mirroring how TokenContrastTest already proves mediaProgress against the bento scrim composite. Contrast is modelled on the sweep, then proven by that pin — not assumed.
  • Discrete pinned pills (the transport glyph backing) use --glyph-fill over the band, mirroring the Trust snooze-chip pinned model.
  • Under OS reduce-transparency, pinned pill alpha → 1.0 (RovaTrustTokens.pinContainerAlphaFor), same as the Trust surfaces.

Nothing on this shell carries meaning by motion. Every animated affordance has a static equivalent; disabling motion never removes information. No motion that competes with the footage (quiet background recordings — chrome motion IS the distraction).

07

Accessibility

The floor, not a pass. WCAG 2.2 AA by default (ADR-0020).
Preserved (already strong)
  • Play/pause + speed = Role.Button, 48dp, dynamic content descriptions.
  • Seekbar fully instrumented: progressBarRangeInfo, stateDescription, setProgress, prev/next customActions, throttled polite live-region, decorative ticks cleared.
  • Auto-hide suppressed under TalkBack.
  • Over-media focus indicator is white (--focus-ring), legible on any frame — not the accent.
Required by this shell
  • Every gesture has a visible on-screen control (gestures are accelerators).
  • Top title + sub-title grouped into one semantic node (currently ungrouped).
  • All over-media ink pairs must clear AA against the worst-case band composite over bright footage — proven by a new scrim-composite contrast pin (the TokenContrastTest/mediaProgress precedent: a token asserted against a scrim composite, ×palettes), not by per-literal tuning and not by borrowing the opaque-surface Trust sweep. The dimmed inks (--media-ink-dim .48, --media-ink-body .55) are the ones to pin, since they sit where the gradient has faded.
  • 48dp minimum targets on every interactive glyph (--target).
  • (Loading/Unavailable live-region announcements & reactive touch-exploration are owned by player-states.html — out of core scope, flagged here for continuity.)
08

Responsive behavior

Phone-first, portrait. Landscape/fullscreen is rejected in V1 (backlog §5.4).
  • Video: full-bleed, ContentScale.Fit for the poster; the surface letterboxes the footage's own aspect (4:3 DualShot / merged 9:16). Fit/fill toggle is a separate spec — core assumes Fit.
  • Chrome bands: full-width; padding on the 16dp edge margin (--edge-margin). Top band fixed 96dp; bottom band hugs content height.
  • Insets: bands extend under the status/nav bars (edge-to-edge, bottom nav hidden); interactive content stays inside safe insets.
  • Small heights: the three bottom children never wrap or overlap the hero; on very short screens the InfoRow's second line (wall-clock) is the first to be de-prioritised — but core assumes a standard phone.
  • No landscape layout in V1; orientation stays portrait-locked for the player route (rejected-set, evaluated 2026-06-20).
09

Implementation notes (for the later Compose transcription)

Non-binding until freeze; captures the transcription intent.
  • Token migration is the deliverable. Replace the 11 Color.White.copy(alpha) literals + Color.Black gradients in PlayerScreen.kt with the F1/F2 tokens (mapping table §02). Same rendered pixels.
  • Add the scrim-composite contrast pin (§07): a pure JVM test asserting --media-ink-dim/--media-ink-body clear AA over the worst-case band-over-bright-video composite — modelled on TokenContrastTest.mediaProgress. This is the shell's contrast guarantee; do NOT reuse the opaque-surface Trust sweep as the proof.
  • Backbone untouched. PlayerViewModel, PlayerEngine/Ledger (ADR-0038), PlayerUriResolver (ADR-0037), WallClockTimeline/SegmentedTimelineMath (ADR-0032) — read-only. This is chrome only.
  • Delete the dead docstrings — the phantom "5-button row (Trim, −10s, Play, +10s, Edit)" at PlayerScreen.kt:90-98 / PlayerViewModel.kt:57-60 describes a UI that does not exist. Remove during transcription so the next designer isn't misled.
  • The route already wraps in RovaDarkSurface + pinned LocalGlassEnvironment but PlayerScreen never consumes it — wire the shell to actually read the pinned environment instead of re-deriving black.
  • Chrome show/hide stays behind AutoHideChromePolicy; do not hardcode timing in the composable.
  • Over-media contrast pins go into the existing sweep test family (no new gate — protection = pure token/contrast JVM tests, per the ADR-0036/0038 precedent).
